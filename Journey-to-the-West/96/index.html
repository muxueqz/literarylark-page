Early the next morning the king ordered that painters make portraits of the countenances of the four holy monks to be kept in the Sino-Barbarian Hal .

The princess was also invited to come out from the throne hal in her new finery to thank the Tang Priest and the other three for saving her from her suffering. When she had thanked them the Tang Priest took his leave of the king to continue his journey West. The king refused to let them go, but ordered great banquets at which they feasted for five or six days. The idiot real y was given a good time, and he enjoyed put ing as much food in his stomach as he possibly could. When the king saw how determined they were to visit the Buddha he realized that no mat er how hard he tried he would not be able to keep them. He had two hundred ingots of gold and silver brought out, as wel as a tray of jewels for each of them as an expression of thanks. Master and disciples refused to accept anything. The king then 1512

ordered the royal carriage prepared, invited the master to enter it, and instructed officials to escort them a long way. The queens, consorts, officials and common people al kowtowed endlessly in thanks. As they went along the way their monks al came out to kowtow to them in farewel ; none of them could bear to be parted from the travelers. Seeing that the people seeing them off were unwil ing to turn back. Monkey had no option but to make a spel with his hands and blow a magic breath in the direction of the trigram of the wind, Xun, so that a dark wind stopped al the escorts from seeing them. Only then did the travelers get away. This was indeed a case of Washing away the waves of gratitude their natures returned to the end; Leaving the sea of gold they were aware of true emptiness.
